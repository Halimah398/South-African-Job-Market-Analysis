# South-African-Job-Market-Analysis
This project presents a comprehensive data analysis of the South African job market using Microsoft Excel, with a focus on uncovering patterns in employment demand, salary distribution, and workforce trends. 
<img width="1556" height="673" alt="South Africa Job Market Trends" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/12cf004e-14e4-4c5e-884d-110c1710dd34" />
The dataset contains structured information on job postings across multiple industries, cities, and experience levels, including key variables such as salary ranges, demand index, number of job openings, education requirements, and skills. Using Excel-based analytical techniques such as Pivot Tables, data cleaning, transformation, and dashboard development, the project explores how labour demand translates into employment opportunities and compensation outcomes.

Key insights from the analysis reveal that job demand does not increase linearly with job openings, indicating possible market saturation or skill mismatches at higher demand levels. Employment opportunities are heavily concentrated in urban centers, with cities like Durban and Johannesburg leading in job availability. The study also highlights that technical roles such as Product Manager, DevOps Engineer, and Data Scientist command the highest salaries, reinforcing the growing importance of digital and analytical skills in the modern labour market.

Furthermore, the analysis shows that experience level is a strong determinant of salary growth, with senior roles earning significantly more than entry-level positions. Permanent employment remains the dominant job type, reflecting organizational preference for long-term workforce stability. Additionally, higher educational qualifications are associated with better salary outcomes, although the returns vary across industries and job roles.

The project delivers actionable recommendations, including the need for targeted upskilling programs, alignment of educational curricula with industry demand, and strategic investment in high-growth sectors such as technology and construction. It also emphasizes the importance of geographic mobility and skill specialization for job seekers aiming to maximize employment opportunities.

Overall, this project demonstrates strong capabilities in data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), data visualization, and business insight generation using Excel. It highlights the ability to translate complex datasets into meaningful insights that support strategic decision-making in workforce development.
